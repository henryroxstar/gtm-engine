# Instagram Optimization Playbook

**For: GTM**
**Scope: followers, virality, engagement on Instagram — tuned to the active profile's content pillars and ICP personas (see the profile's `social-tuning.md` and `icp-personas.md`), plus the broader tech-curious + early-career talent audience, across the profile's primary regions.**

> **Content pillars (shared across all platform docs):**
> Your three topic territories — **`<Pillar A>`**, **`<Pillar B>`**, **`<Pillar C>`** — defined once
> per tenant in the active profile (`PROFILE.md` → `content_pillars`, expanded in `social-tuning.md`).
>
> The company's **positioning wedge** sits where these pillars meet — read it from the active profile's `social-tuning.md`.

**Be realistic about Instagram's role.** Instagram is not where enterprise AI buyers make decisions — that's LinkedIn, and the live technical/policy discourse is X. **Instagram's job here is top-of-funnel: brand-building, reach to non-followers (Reels), humanizing the company, explaining hard concepts simply, and recruiting/employer-brand** (it skews younger and is strong in APAC, esp. India/SEA). Treat it as the **awareness + brand layer**, not the conversion layer. If bandwidth is tight, Instagram is third priority behind LinkedIn and X — but its Reels reach to *new* audiences is genuinely valuable for category-building around the company's wedge.

---

## 0. The 10-second version

1. **Reels are the discovery engine.** They reach ~2x more non-followers than static posts. Use Reels to grow.
2. **Carousels are the engagement/teaching engine.** ~109% more engagement per person reached than Reels. Use them to go deep and earn saves.
3. **Run both:** ~2 Reels/week for reach + 3–5 feed posts (mostly carousels) for depth. 4–7 posts/week total gets the strongest algorithmic support.
4. **Saves + DM shares are the top signals.** "Sends per reach" (people DMing your post) is the strongest reach driver; design for shareability.
5. **Reel completion rate is king.** Get past the 3-second mark, then hold to the end. Hook hard, keep it tight (30–90s sweet spot; 7–15s for trend-jacks).
6. **Caption SEO matters now.** Instagram reads captions/keywords to classify and surface content in search & suggestions. Write real, keyword-aware captions, not just emojis.
7. **First hour matters.** Early likes/comments/shares signal quality and widen distribution.
8. **Post Tue/Wed/Fri;** lunch (11am–1pm) and evening (7–9pm) for carousels, mornings/evenings for Reels.
9. **Hook on slide 1 / second 1.** First slide and first frame decide everything.
10. **Consistency + one visual identity.** A recognizable template/brand system compounds recall.

## 1. How the Instagram algorithm works in 2026

Instagram doesn't have one algorithm — it ranks each surface (Feed, Reels, Stories, Explore, Search) differently. For growth, two surfaces matter most: **Reels (reach new people)** and **Feed/carousels (engage the people you reach).**

**The signals that drive distribution, by weight:**

| Signal | Where it matters | What you do |
|---|---|---|
| **Sends/DM shares per reach** | strongest for reaching *new* audiences | Make content people want to DM a colleague ("you need to see this"). |
| **Saves per reach** | strong; especially carousels | Reference-grade, screenshot-worthy value. |
| **Watch time / completion rate** | the dominant Reels signal | Tight edits, strong hook, no dead air, loop-ability. |
| **Likes per reach** | moderate | Baseline quality signal. |
| **Comments** | moderate + extends reach | Ask a real question; reply to build threads. |
| **Caption/keyword relevance** | search & suggested ranking | Caption SEO: say what it's about, in words. |
| **First-hour engagement** | distribution trigger | Post at peak; be available to reply. |

**Reels vs Carousels — they do different jobs:**
- **Reels = discovery.** ~2x reach to non-followers. This is your *acquisition* format.
- **Carousels = engagement & retention.** ~109% more engagement per person reached; high saves; keep your existing audience close and teach. This is your *authority* format.
- **The winning mix is both:** Reels to bring strangers in, carousels to convert attention into following + understanding.

**Other 2026 realities:**
- **Original content is favored** over reposted/watermarked (avoid recognizable TikTok watermarks).
- **Hashtags are minor now** — 3–5 relevant ones at most; caption keywords do the classifying work.
- **Links** live in bio / link sticker (Stories) — there's no in-caption clickable link, which actually keeps the platform's link-penalty problem off your back. Drive to "link in bio."

## 2. When to post

**Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday (most consistently cited). Tue–Thu broadly strong.

**Best windows (audience-local):**
- **Mornings:** 6–9am (commute/coffee scroll).
- **Lunch:** 11am–1pm — *best for carousels* (people have time to swipe).
- **Evening:** 7–9pm — strong for both; **6–11pm especially strong for Reels** (Wed/Thu peak).

**By format:**
- **Reels:** 9am–12pm Mon–Thu, *and* evenings 6–11pm. Wednesday peaks.
- **Carousels:** 11am–1pm and 7–9pm (swipe-time windows).

**Two clocks (APAC + US):**
- **APAC** (India/SEA huge on IG): evenings 7–10pm local are prime; mornings also work. Localized/visual content over-indexes here.
- **US:** lunch + 7–9pm ET.
- Posting at peak vs off-peak can swing reach ~20–30%, so respect the windows — but the **hook and completion rate matter far more than the exact minute.**

---

## 3. Cadence

- **4–7 posts/week** gets the strongest algorithmic support.
- **Ideal mix: ~2 Reels/week + 3–5 feed posts** (lean carousel-heavy).
- **Stories: near-daily** (low-effort, keeps you top-of-feed with your warm audience; use polls/questions/link stickers).
- Consistency > bursts. A steady 4–5/week beats 12 one week and 0 the next.

---

## 4. Format priority stack

1. **Reels — top of the stack for growth.** Only format that reliably reaches non-followers at scale. Make these your acquisition bet.
2. **Carousels — top for engagement/authority.** Saves + dwell; your teaching format for the pillars.
3. **Stories — relationship + utility.** Daily presence, polls/questions, behind-the-scenes, "link in bio" driver, event coverage.
4. **Single image / quote card.** Fine for milestones, quote graphics, announcements; lowest reach ceiling.
5. **Collab posts** (with partners, events, creators) — share audiences; great for category-building.

## 5. Platform constraints & specs

**Reels**
- **1080 × 1920 px (9:16).** Full-screen vertical.
- **Length: 30–90s sweet spot** for engagement/views; **7–15s** for trend-jacks; keep it as tight as the idea allows.
- **Captions burned in** (most watch muted). **Hook in the first 1–2 seconds.** Design for the loop (end ties to start).
- Cover/thumbnail: add a clear text cover so it reads in-grid.

**Carousels**
- **1080 × 1350 px (4:5) per slide** — all slides same ratio.
- **7–10 slides outperform 3–4** (more dwell = stronger signal). 
- Slide 1 = hook; middle = one idea/slide; last = CTA + follow prompt. Big legible text.

**Feed images**
- 1080×1350 (4:5, takes most screen — preferred), 1080×1080 (square), or 1080×566 (landscape, weakest).

**Stories**
- 1080×1920 (9:16). Use interactive stickers (poll, quiz, question, link) — they boost engagement and feed the algorithm.

**Captions**
- Up to 2,200 chars; first ~125 show before "more." Front-load the hook + keywords. Write for **caption SEO** — name the topic plainly ("AI agents," "AI Act," "verifiable AI").
- 3–5 relevant hashtags max.

**Profile**
- Bio: what the company does in plain language + the pillar keywords + a clear "link in bio" CTA. Name field is searchable — include a keyword (e.g., "<Company> — <core pillar keyword>"). Highlights: organized by pillar (Trust / Policy / Agents / Team).

## 6. How to craft content

**Reel anatomy:**
```
[0–2s]  HOOK — visual + text + spoken, all at once. The boldest claim/question.
[2–8s]  TENSION — why this matters / the stakes / the promise.
[8–N]   PAYOFF — the insight/steps/demo, fast cuts, on-screen text reinforcing.
[end]   CTA — a follow promise naming the payoff of your wedge + loop back to hook.
```
- Completion rate is everything: cut every dead second. If a beat doesn't earn attention, delete it.
- Show, don't tell: a 5-second agent demo or a simple animated diagram beats talking-head abstraction.
- One idea per Reel.

**Carousel anatomy:**
```
Slide 1: HOOK — the single boldest promise/claim (this is the thumbnail).
Slide 2: stakes — why it matters / cost of getting it wrong.
Slides 3–8: one idea per slide (steps, do/don't, myth/truth, before/after).
Slide 9: recap — the whole framework on one screen (= saves).
Slide 10: CTA — follow + a question that drives comments.
```

**Caption anatomy (both formats):**
```
[Hook line — repeats/extends the visual hook, keyword-rich]
[2–4 short lines of value or context]
[CTA question]
[3–5 relevant hashtags]
```

## 7. Content angles for the pillars (made visual)

Instagram forces simplification — that's a feature. Translate the pillars into accessible, visual stories (the examples below are illustrative — swap in your own domain):

- **`<Pillar A>`:** "Would you let an AI sign this for you? 🧵 in slides," provenance explained with a simple diagram, "3 questions to ask before you trust an AI's output," myth-vs-truth carousels.
- **`<Pillar B>`:** "The EU AI Act in 8 slides (no legalese)," "What 'high-risk AI' actually means," a Reel reacting to a policy headline in plain language.
- **`<Pillar C>`:** short demo Reels of agents doing (and failing at) tasks, "How AI agents actually work, explained simply," "What could go wrong when an agent acts on your behalf" + the guardrail.
- **Human/brand:** team intros, "building in public," event coverage, behind-the-scenes of building the trust layer — these humanize and recruit.

Keep one visual template system so every post is instantly recognizable as the company's.

## 8. Leading voices — what works on IG for technical/AI brands

Instagram has fewer pure AI-policy thought leaders than X/LinkedIn (it's a consumer surface), so the model here is **"how do technical/B2B brands and AI educators win on a visual platform":**

- **AI educators / explainer accounts** turn complex AI into snackable carousels and Reels — they win on *clarity and consistency*, not credentials. Borrow their simplification.
- **Founder/creator faces** out-perform faceless brand accounts — a recognizable human (founder, DevRel) explaining things builds trust and following.
- **Brands that win** have a **distinct visual identity**, post **education + behind-the-scenes**, and use **Reels for reach, carousels for depth.**
- **Demos travel.** For a "show the product working" pillar especially, short "watch this agent do X" clips are inherently shareable — the single most IG-native asset the company can make.

**Transferable behaviors (same everywhere):** own a lane, teach in repeatable formats, be consistent, show a human, and design for shares/saves.

## 9. Regional notes (APAC vs US)

- **APAC is Instagram's powerhouse for reach** — India and SEA have massive, highly engaged IG audiences, strong on Reels and visual storytelling. Localized content (language, idioms, local context) sees materially higher engagement; multi-image/visual content over-indexes. Evenings (7–10pm local) are prime.
- **US:** smaller relative AI-on-IG audience than APAC, but valuable for brand/recruiting; lunch + evening windows.
- **Practical:** make APAC-localized variants of your best Reels/carousels (subtitles, local framing); schedule to APAC evenings; keep a US evening slot for brand reach. Reels are your cross-region reach vehicle.

2. `This AI agent just did 6 things on its own. Here's the scary part. 👇`

5. `3 questions to ask before you trust any AI output.`
6. `Everyone's building AI agents. Almost no one can prove what they did.`

### B. Carousel skeletons
**"N questions/mistakes" deck**
```
Slide 1: 3 questions to ask before you trust an AI agent
Slide 2: Why this matters now (agents are acting on your behalf)
Slides 3–5: one question per slide + what a good answer looks like
Slide 6: the checklist, all on one screen (save this)
Slide 7: Follow @<handle> for [the payoff of your wedge] + "Which would you ask first?"
```
**"Explainer" deck**
```
Slide 1: How AI agents actually work, in 7 slides
Slide 2: the problem they solve
Slides 3–6: one component/step per slide + simple diagram
Slide 7: the full picture + CTA
```

### C. Caption template
```
[Keyword-rich hook line — e.g., "AI agents are great — until you have to prove what they did."]

[2–3 short value lines.]

Save this for the next time someone says "just trust the AI."

What would you add? 👇

#AITrust #AgenticAI #AIagents #AIpolicy #responsibleAI
```

### D. Story sequence (near-daily)
```
1. Poll: "Do you trust AI agents to act on your behalf? Yes / Not yet"
2. Result + 1 insight
3. "Swipe up to the new carousel / link in bio"
4. Behind-the-scenes: building the trust layer
```

### E. CTA bank
- "Follow for [the payoff of your wedge, in the reader's words]."
- "Save this for your next AI project."
- "Send this to someone shipping agents."
- "Which one surprised you? 👇"

## 11. Metrics — track these

- **Reach split: followers vs non-followers** (Reels should pull lots of non-followers — that's growth).
- **Sends/shares per reach** (the top reach driver — design for it).
- **Saves per reach** (carousels should lead).
- **Reel completion rate / average watch time** (the make-or-break Reels signal; if people drop at 3s, fix the hook).
- **Follower growth rate** and **profile visits** (leading indicator).
- **Per-format performance** — confirm Reels-for-reach / carousels-for-engagement holds for you.

Review every 2 weeks initially, then monthly.

## 13. Company cheat sheet

- **Role:** awareness + brand + recruiting (top-of-funnel). Not the conversion channel — that's LinkedIn/X.
- **Reels for reach, carousels for depth.** Run both; ~2 Reels + 3–5 feed/week.
- **Make demos.** Short "watch an agent do/fail at X" clips are the most IG-native, shareable asset for a product-in-action pillar.
- **APAC-first reach.** India/SEA are your biggest, most engaged IG audiences — localize and post to their evenings.
