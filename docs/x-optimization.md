**Why X matters for these pillars:** X is *the* real-time home of technical discourse in a fast-moving category — AI is the canonical example. The researchers, founders, alignment/safety people, and agent-builders who define such a space live here and argue in public. For pillars like these, X is where narratives form first and where credibility with technical + policy audiences is won. LinkedIn is for depth and decision-makers; **X is for being part of the live discourse and earning peer credibility.**

## 0. The 10-second version

1. **Replies are the game.** A reply that the author replies back to is weighted ~75x a like; replies broadly carry ~15x the weight of likes. Conversation > broadcast.
2. **Win the first 30–60 minutes.** Early engagement velocity is the quality stamp that triggers wider distribution.
3. **No outbound links in the main post.** Off-platform links cut reach ~50–90%. Put links in a reply, or skip them.
4. **Threads > single posts for depth;** native video for reach. Threads reportedly ~3x the engagement of single tweets.
5. **Reply 30x/day to bigger accounts in-niche.** This is the #1 follower-growth lever on X. Be early, be smart, be additive.
6. **Premium (verified) is close to mandatory** for serious reach — verified accounts get a meaningful ranking/visibility boost and top-of-thread reply placement.
7. **Pick one lane and stay in it.** The algorithm builds your topic profile; consistency inside your content pillars compounds.
8. **Be constructive, not combative.** Sentiment is scored; negative/inflammatory posts get throttled. Sharp ≠ hostile.
9. **Post Tue–Thu, ~9–11am; reuse evenings (8–11pm) for amplifying winners.**
10. **Volume + consistency:** ~1–3 posts/day + 1–2 threads/week + daily replies. X rewards being present, every day.

## 1. How the X algorithm works in 2026

X open-sourced much of its ranking, so this is unusually well understood. The "For You" feed runs a three-stage pipeline:

1. **Candidate sourcing** — ~1,500 candidate posts pulled from ~500M daily, roughly half from accounts you follow (in-network) and half out-of-network (discovery). Out-of-network is how you reach new followers.
2. **Neural-network ranking** — each candidate scored on predicted engagement, heavily weighting *replies and conversation*.
3. **Heuristics & filters** — diversity (not too many from one author), visibility filtering, sentiment, and quality screens.

**The engagement weighting (the part that should change your behavior):**

| Action | Relative algorithmic weight | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Reply that author replies to | ~75x | Start conversations; reply to your repliers. |
| Reply | ~15x a like | Ask things people answer; reply on others' posts. |
| Repost | high | Earn reshares with a strong, quotable line. |
| Like | baseline (~1x) | Nice, but the weakest real signal. |
| Profile click → follow / dwell | strong | A great post makes people click your profile; your bio must convert. |
| **Outbound link click-away** | **negative** | Links suppress reach ~50–90%. Avoid in main post. |
| Negative/"show less"/mute/block | strongly negative | Combative or baity content gets buried. |

**What this means practically:**
- **Follower count barely matters; engagement rate does.** A 500-follower account pulling 50 replies/post out-distributes a 10k account pulling 20 likes.
- **Conversation depth is everything.** Posts that spark back-and-forth threads win. Build replies into the design of every post.
- **First 30–60 minutes set the ceiling.** Early velocity tells the ranker your post is worth showing out-of-network.
- **Sentiment is scored.** Constructive, additive content is boosted; dunking and rage-bait are throttled (and burn trust with the technical/policy crowd you want).
- **Premium/verified boost is real** — 2x–4x reach is commonly cited, plus verified replies sort to the top of threads (huge for the reply strategy below).

## 2. When to post

**Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (Wednesday most often ranked #1, then Tue, then Thu).

**Best windows (audience-local):**
- **Primary:** weekday mornings **9–11am** (top cited slots: Tue 9am, Wed 9–10am).
- **Secondary / broad:** **12–6pm** Tue–Thu.
- **Amplification:** **8–11pm** is strong for *reposting/quoting a post that already did well* — ride the evening scroll.

**The AI-discourse caveat:** for these pillars, *news-reactivity beats the clock.* When a major model drops, a regulation lands, or a safety story breaks, the highest-leverage move is a sharp, fast take **within the first hour or two** — timeliness outweighs the "ideal" slot. Keep dry-powder takes ready.

**Two clocks (APAC + US):**
- **US:** 9–11am ET, Tue–Thu. The bulk of the AI/policy commentariat is US-time-zoned.
- **APAC:** 9–11am and an evening 8–11pm slot (India/SEA active post-work). A morning-SGT post also catches US evening.
- A post around **9–10am ET** hits the US AI crowd at peak and APAC late-evening simultaneously — often the single best slot for global AI discourse.

## 3. Cadence

- **~1–3 original posts/day**, every day. X rewards constant presence more than any other platform here.
- **1–2 threads/week** (your depth pieces).
- **~20–30 replies/day** on larger in-niche accounts — non-negotiable for growth.
- **Quote-post** others' big posts with genuine added insight (borrows their audience).
- Don't over-automate. Scheduled posts are fine for the backbone, but the replies and reactive takes must be live and human.

## 4. Format priority stack

1. **Replies (to bigger accounts) — your top growth engine.** Not "content" per se, but where new followers actually come from. Early + additive + in-niche.
2. **Native video (<2:20).** Best discovery format; algorithm favors on-platform video. Hook in 2 sec, captions on.
3. **Threads.** Best for depth, teaching, and "saveable" value; each tweet can be liked/reposted individually = more surface area. Great for explainers across all three pillars.
4. **Single strong text post / hot take.** Fast, high-ceiling for opinion. 70–150 chars often over-performs for punchy takes; longer when the substance warrants.
5. **Image / chart / screenshot post.** A clean diagram or annotated screenshot of a paper/reg earns reposts.
6. **Long-form (Premium "Articles," up to 25k chars).** Good for canonical pieces you'll link back to; lower reach ceiling than threads but keeps people on-platform (better than an outbound link).
7. **Outbound-link post.** Bottom — only when the click matters more than the reach, and even then put the link in a reply.

## 5. Platform constraints & specs

**Posts**
- **280 characters** standard; **25,000** for Premium (truncates in-timeline, expands on tap).
- Punchy single takes: **~70–150 chars** often perform best. Don't pad.
- 1–2 hashtags max (X doesn't reward hashtag stacking; often better with zero).

**Threads**
- Each tweet = its own 280 chars (Premium: more). Auto-numbering ("1/", "🧵") eats into the count.
- **Structure:** tweet 1 = the whole hook + promise (this is the only one most people see); tweets 2–N = one idea each, content-dense; final tweet = recap + CTA + (optional) the link.
- Keep threads tight: **5–9 tweets** for most; go longer only if every tweet earns its place.

**Video**
- <2:20 for best initial distribution; vertical or square; burn-in captions; hook in first 2 seconds; native upload.

**Images**
- 16:9 (1600×900) or square (1080×1080). Legible on mobile. Diagrams/charts > stock.

**Profile (convert the profile-clicks your posts earn)**
- Bio: who you help + on what (name your pillars) + a reason to follow. Pinned post = your best thread or a sharp POV. Header image = clear positioning. Premium/verified on.

## 6. How to craft a post

**Single post anatomy:**
```
[Hook / claim — the whole point in line 1]
[Optional: 1–2 lines of reasoning or a twist]
[Implicit or explicit invitation to reply]
```
- Front-load the take. No "A thread on…" throat-clearing on single posts.
- Specific > vague. Name the model, the reg, the failure.
- Leave a hook for replies — a question, a "change my mind," a gap.
- Constructive edge. Contrarian is great; cruel is throttled.

**Thread anatomy:**
```
1/ [Hook: bold claim + promise of payoff. Must stand alone.]
2/ [Set the stakes / why now]
3..N/ [One idea per tweet — step, example, data point, screenshot]
N/ [Recap + CTA: "Follow @handle for more on [your lane]" + question]
(reply to your own last tweet with the source link, if any)
```

**Rules:** hook tweet does 80% of the work; every tweet must be independently interesting (they get shown separately); end with a clean CTA; reply-link only at the end.

## 8. The reply strategy (most important growth tactic on X)

This is how small accounts become known. Daily routine:

1. **Build a "target list" of 20–40 larger in-niche accounts** (AI researchers, safety/alignment voices, agent-builders, AI-policy people, relevant founders/journalists).
2. **Turn on notifications** for the top ~10. Be among the **first 5–10 replies** — early replies on a big post get massive impressions, especially if you're verified (top-of-thread placement).
3. **Make every reply additive:** a sharp insight, a relevant example, a respectful counter, a useful link to a primary source. Never "great post 🔥".
4. **~20–30 replies/day.** Track which earn profile-clicks/follows; lean into those accounts.
5. **Quote-post** the best ones with your own take to put it in front of *your* audience too.

Constructive, expert replies on big accounts are the single highest-ROI activity on X for a new authority brand.

The AI conversation on X clusters into a few archetypes. Study these patterns (not just follower counts):

| Archetype | What they post | What to borrow |

| **Safety/alignment researchers** | governance arguments, risk framings, reactions to papers/reports (e.g., alignment & safety debates around autonomous agency) | credible, sourced, measured takes inside `<Pillar A>` + `<Pillar B>`; thread explainers |

**The transferable behaviors (identical to what wins everywhere):** own one lane, post a POV not just news, build in public with real specifics, be relentlessly consistent, and *live in replies.* On X specifically, **speed on breaking AI news** and **being verified for reply visibility** are the two X-native multipliers.

**Follow-and-engage list:** maintain ~10–15 accounts across categories (researchers, founders, educators, practitioners, policy) to triangulate the conversation and to have warm reply targets.

## 10. Regional notes (APAC vs US)

- **US** is the center of gravity for the AI/policy discourse and runs on US business hours; expect the sharpest, fastest, most opinion-driven conversation. Direct takes and clear POV travel.
- **APAC** (Singapore HQ + India/SEA): strong evening activity (8–11pm local); audiences respond well to localized framing and community tone. India in particular has a large, active builder community on X — localized, builder-first content lands.
- **Practical:** anchor the flagship AI-discourse posts to US morning, and run a localized/builder-oriented evening cadence for APAC. When news breaks, post immediately regardless of clock — the cycle doesn't wait.

## 11. Templates

### A. Single-post hooks
1. `"The agent handled it" is not a control. Here's what actually counts as one.`
2. `Hot take: most "trustworthy AI" claims can't survive one question — [the question].`
3. `Everyone's shipping agents. Almost nobody can prove what they did. That's the whole problem.`
4. `Read the [AI Act / report] so you don't have to. The [N] lines that matter: …`
5. `Unpopular opinion: [pillar belief]. Here's why I think the consensus is wrong.`
6. `New [model/agent/reg] dropped. The part everyone's missing: …`
7. `If your AI can't show its work, you don't have trust — you have hope.`

### B. Thread openers (tweet 1/)
1. `1/ How multi-agent systems actually fail in production — and the guardrail for each. A thread 🧵`
2. `1/ The EU AI Act in plain English for people who build, not lawyers. 🧵`
3. `1/ "Verifiable AI" gets thrown around a lot. Here's what it actually requires, step by step 🧵`
4. `1/ I've watched a lot of agent demos. Here's the trust checklist I now run every one through 🧵`

### C. Reply templates (for daily engagement)
- Add-value: `The piece I'd add: [specific insight]. We're seeing [concrete example] when [condition].`
- Respectful counter: `Agree on [X] — though [Y] complicates it. In [context], [evidence] points the other way.`
- Question that extends: `This is the core tension. How are you handling [specific edge case] — verification at runtime or after?`
- Source-add: `Primary source for anyone digging in: [what it is]. The relevant part is [section].`

### D. Quote-post template
`The point in [quote] generalizes: [your one-line extension tied to one of your pillars]. Worth reading.`

### E. CTA bank (end of threads)
- "Building agents you actually have to trust? Follow @handle — that's all I post about."
- "What would you add — what's failed for you in production?"
- "Agree / disagree? Tell me where this breaks."

## 12. Metrics — track these

- **Engagement rate** (replies + reposts ÷ impressions), not raw followers.
- **Replies and reply-depth** (back-and-forth threads = the signal that matters).
- **Profile clicks → follows** (your post-to-profile conversion; if high impressions but low follows, fix the bio/pinned).
- **Out-of-network impression %** (X analytics) — shows whether you're reaching new audiences.
- **Per-format performance** — confirm threads vs single vs video for *your* audience.
- **Which reply-targets drive follows** — double down.

Review every 2 weeks initially; X moves fast, so iterate quickly.

## 14. Company cheat sheet
