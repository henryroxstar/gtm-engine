## 0. The 10-second version (do these and you've captured 80% of the upside)

1. **Post from people, not the logo.** Personal profiles out-reach the company page by roughly 5x (some vendors claim 8x). Build founder + employee voices; use the company page for amplification, jobs, and product news only.
2. **Lead with carousels (PDF documents).** Highest engagement format on the platform by a wide margin. Aim for 1–2/week per active voice.
3. **Win the first 210 characters.** That's the "see more" cutoff. The hook decides reach.
4. **Win the first 60 minutes.** Early dwell time + quality comments in the first hour is the single biggest reach lever. Be live to reply.
5. **No outbound links in the post body.** External links cut reach ~50–60%. Put the link in a comment or in the post only after it has matured (or skip it).
6. **Drop the hashtags, drop the engagement bait.** In 2026 the algorithm reads your full copy, not tags. Hashtags now slightly *hurt*. "Comment YES" bait is detected and suppressed.
7. **Post Tue–Thu, mid-morning local; add an evening slot.** Evening (3–8pm) is rising fast and is especially strong in APAC.
8. **Comment daily on bigger accounts in your niche.** 15–20 min/day of smart commenting is the cheapest follower-growth channel that exists.
9. **Optimize for dwell time and saves, not likes.** Long-ish, scannable, genuinely useful posts beat quick-hit one-liners.
10. **Be consistent.** 3–5 posts/week per voice. Consistency beats polish; accounts at 5x/week grow dramatically faster than 1–2x/week.

## 1. How the LinkedIn algorithm works in 2026 (and what changed)

The mental model that matters this year:

**It's an interest graph, not a relationship graph.** LinkedIn now shows content based on *topics a user engages with*, not just who they're connected to. This is why a sharp post from a stranger can reach you. Practical consequence: **topic consistency builds "Topic Authority."** If you post inside the same few topic territories repeatedly, the algorithm learns to push your content to people who engage with those topics — even outside your network. Scattershot topics dilute this. Stay inside your content pillars; resist the urge to post off-topic.

**The ranking objective shifted from viral reach to "depth and authority."** The system rewards content that creates meaningful engagement among the right audience, not raw volume. A post read by 2,000 of the right people with strong dwell time will out-convert a 50,000-impression fluff post — and the algorithm increasingly agrees.

**The metrics that actually drive distribution, in order:**

| Signal | Why it matters | What you do about it |
|---|---|---|
| **Dwell time** | How long people stop on your post. The strongest single signal. | Longer scannable posts, carousels, "save-worthy" depth. A 30-sec read beats 50 fast likes. |
| **Comment quality** | Substantive comments weighted heavily; thread replies extend reach. | Ask real questions; reply to every comment with a follow-up question to deepen threads. |
| **Early velocity (first 60 min)** | Engagement in the first hour determines whether it gets pushed wider. | Post when your audience is on; be available to reply for 60–90 min. |
| **Saves & shares** | Strong intent signals; carousels win here. | Make reference-grade content people bookmark. |
| **Reshares with commentary** | Worth more than a bare repost. | Ask advocates to add a line when they share. |

**What's penalized or dead in 2026:**

- **Outbound links in the body:** ~50–60% less reach. The "link in first comment" trick is now also detected and discounted. Best practice: no link, or add it to a comment a few hours later once reach is established.
- **Hashtags:** No longer used to classify content. Posts *without* hashtags now slightly outperform (≈5–10%). Use 0, or at most 1–2 genuinely relevant ones. Don't stack them.
- **Engagement bait:** "Type 1 if you agree," "Comment GUIDE and I'll DM you" — pattern-detected and suppressed. Genuine questions are fine.
- **Engagement pods:** Pod detection reportedly hit ~97% accuracy. Coordinated pod engagement is effectively dead and can hurt you. Build real relationships instead.
- **Company-page broadcast:** Company-page organic reach fell ~60% since 2024. The platform is steering brand reach toward paid. Don't rely on it for organic distribution.

> ⚠️ **Source-quality note:** specific percentages (e.g., "596% more engagement," "8x," "340%") circulate widely across vendor blogs and are often re-quotes of a single underlying study. Treat the *direction* as reliable (carousels > video > image > text > links; personal > company; early engagement matters most) and the *exact multiples* as directional, not gospel. Validate against your own analytics once you have ~20+ posts.

## 2. When to post

### Global baseline (audience-timezone-relative)
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday.
- **Best core window:** ~10am–2pm local, with **Tuesday 10–11am** the most consistently cited single best hour.
- **Worst:** weekends (≈50% less reach), and to a lesser degree Mondays and Fridays for serious content.
- **Friday:** if you post, keep it lighter (culture, team, reflection) and aim 9–11am.

### The 2026 shift: evenings are rising
Peak windows are moving later. **3–8pm now generates strong engagement** as professionals catch up after work / on commutes. Top emerging slots: **Wed 4pm, Fri 3–4pm.** This mirrors Instagram/TikTok behavior bleeding into LinkedIn.

### Post to TWO clocks
When the audience straddles two primary regions — here, APAC and the US — don't average them into a mushy middle. **Run two slots and tag content to the region it's for.** (The active profile's `social-tuning.md` names its regions.)

| Audience | Best days | Primary slot (local) | Secondary slot (local) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **APAC** (Singapore, India, SEA) | Tue–Thu | 12–3pm | **7–10pm** (Singapore), 5–7pm (India) — evening is genuinely strong here |
| **US** (ET/PT) | Tue–Thu | 10am–12pm ET | 3–5pm ET |

Practical scheduling: a **post around 9–11am Singapore time** catches APAC midday *and* is still the evening-before for the US; a **post around 9–10am ET** catches the US morning and APAC late evening. Pick the clock that matches the post's primary audience and ICP.

## 3. Posting cadence

- **Per active voice: 3–5 posts/week minimum.** Daily is ideal for aggressive growth but only if quality holds. Consistency beats volume-without-quality.
- **Don't post twice in <18–24h from the same profile** — posts cannibalize each other's first-hour velocity.
- **Format mix per week (per voice):** ~1–2 carousels, 1 video, 1–2 text/text+image, occasional poll. Don't post the same format every day.
- **Company page:** 3–4x/week max, treated as an amplification and credibility surface, not the growth engine.

## 4. Format priority stack (what to make, in order)

Ranked by current reach/engagement efficiency on LinkedIn:

1. **Carousels / PDF "document" posts — the king.** Highest engagement and dwell time of any format (commonly cited at ~20%+ engagement rates, multiples above text). Save-worthy, swipe = dwell time, mobile-dominant. **Default to this for substantive content.**
2. **Native video (esp. <60s).** Algorithm is actively pushing video distribution in 2026. Polish is optional — a clear smartphone talking-head explaining one insight works. Always caption (most watch muted). Hook in first 2 seconds.
3. **Text + single strong image / data visual.** Reliable, fast to produce, good for opinion/story posts.
4. **Pure text.** Fine for hot takes and personal stories; lowest ceiling but lowest effort.
5. **Polls.** Cheap reach + comment fuel, but use sparingly — overuse reads as low-effort.
6. **Anything with an outbound link in the body.** Bottom of the stack — only when the click genuinely matters more than the reach.

## 5. Platform constraints & specs (the hard numbers)

**Text posts**
- Max length: **3,000 characters** (incl. spaces, emoji, punctuation).
- **"See more" cutoff:** ~**140 chars on mobile**, ~210 on desktop. Optimize the hook for **mobile (~140)**.
- **Highest-engagement length: ~1,300–2,500 characters** (a full hook→context→insight→CTA arc, ~60–90 sec read). Sub-400-char posts underperform on the metrics that matter now.
- Line breaks are your friend — short paragraphs, lots of white space, scannable.

**Carousels (PDF document posts)**
- Upload as **PDF** (LinkedIn accepts PPTX/DOCX but PDF renders consistently everywhere).
- **Dimensions: 1080 × 1350 px (4:5 portrait) is the strongest** — maximizes vertical screen real estate on mobile = more dwell. 1080×1080 (1:1) acceptable; 1920×1080 (landscape) weakest.
- **Slide count: 5–15** (sweet spot ~8–12). LinkedIn allows up to 300 but short, tight decks win.
- Big text, one idea per slide, an explicit "swipe" cue early, a CTA on the last slide.

**Video**
- Square (1:1) or vertical (4:5 / 9:16) for mobile. Keep <60s for the reach bonus where possible.
- **Burn in captions.** Hook in the first 2 seconds. Native upload, not a YouTube link.

**Images**
- 1200×627 (landscape) or 1080×1350 (portrait). Portrait takes more screen = better. Multi-image posts perform especially well in APAC (≈2x impressions vs single image).

**Profile (do this before any growth push)**
- Headline: outcome + audience, not job title. Banner: clear value prop / CTA. Featured section: best carousel + a way to act. "Creator mode"/follow enabled. Custom URL.

## 6. How to craft a post — the anatomy

Every high-performing post has the same four parts:

```
[1] HOOK            → first 1–2 lines, <140 chars, earns the "see more"
[2] CONTEXT/SETUP   → 1–3 short lines: stakes, tension, or the promise
[3] VALUE BODY      → the payoff: insight, steps, story, evidence. Scannable.
[4] CTA             → one clear question or directive (drives comments)
```

**Hook rules**
- Front-load the most interesting/contrarian/specific thing. No throat-clearing ("I've been thinking lately...").
- Make a promise or create a gap the reader needs to close.
- Specifics > generalities. Numbers, named outcomes, a sharp claim.
- One idea. The hook is not the place for nuance.

**Body rules**
- One line per idea. White space. No walls of text.
- Show, don't assert — example, number, or mini-story.
- Write at a smart-but-busy-reader level. Cut jargon unless your ICP *speaks* it (for developer/security personas, precise technical language builds credibility — match the persona).

**CTA rules**
- Ask ONE genuine question that someone with an opinion can't resist answering.
- Avoid bait. "What's your take?" is weak; "Which of these two approaches have you actually shipped, and what broke?" pulls real comments.

See Section 11 for fill-in-the-blank templates.

## 7. Carousel playbook (your highest-leverage format)

**Structure (8–12 slides):**

1. **Cover / hook slide** — the single boldest promise or claim. Big text. This is your thumbnail; it decides the stop.
2. **Slide 2: the stakes** — why this matters / the cost of getting it wrong.
3. **Slides 3–N: one idea per slide** — steps, framework, do/don't, before/after, myths/truths.
4. **Penultimate slide: the summary** — recap the framework so it's screenshot-able (= saves).
5. **Final slide: CTA** — follow [name] for more on [pillar], + one engagement question. Keep the brand/byline consistent.

**Carousel angles, one per pillar** (the examples below are illustrative — swap in your own domain):
- *`<Pillar A>`:* "[N] mistakes teams make trusting agent output — and how to verify each," "How to prove an AI did what it claims, in [N] slides," "The provenance checklist for AI systems"
- *`<Pillar B>`:* "What the EU AI Act actually requires of you, in [N] slides," "[N] compliance traps in agentic deployments," "High-risk vs limited-risk AI: a builder's decoder"
- *`<Pillar C>`:* "How a multi-agent system actually works, in [N] slides," "[N] failure modes of autonomous agents (and the guardrail for each)," "Agent identity & permissions, explained"
- Evergreen: "[Old way] vs [new way]," "How [concept] actually works" (explainers — builders save these), "[N] things we learned shipping [thing]"

**Caption for a carousel:** still write a full hook + 2–3 lines + CTA in the post text. The caption earns the click into the carousel.

## 8. Visual & image guidance

- **Native, branded, consistent.** A recurring visual system (color, font, layout) makes your content recognizable in-feed and compounds brand recall. (Lock a carousel template — consistent header, footer byline, palette.)
- **Data visuals out-perform stock.** A clean chart, a simple diagram, or an annotated screenshot beats generic imagery every time. For a technical audience, architecture diagrams and flow diagrams earn saves.
- **Faces work.** Posts with a real person (founder, team) tend to out-engage faceless graphics — humans stop for humans.
- **Multi-image posts** are strong, *especially in APAC* (≈2x single-image impressions). Good for event recaps, before/afters, step sequences.
- **Text-on-image** must be legible on a phone. Big, high-contrast, minimal.

## 9. Leading voices — who's effective and what they actually do

### The follower giants (context, not your model)
Bill Gates (~39M), Richard Branson (~19M), Satya Nadella (~12M), Adam Grant (~5M+). Useful to study for *cadence and tone*, but their reach comes from off-platform fame. **Don't model raw follower count — model the operators who built audiences from a standing start in a niche.** That's the relevant comp here.

### What the genuinely effective B2B/tech voices do (the transferable playbook)
Across the creators who built large, *relevant* followings in AI, safety/alignment, AI governance, and dev tooling (e.g., researchers debating alignment and governance, agent-builders posting candidly about agentic engineering, and digital-transformation/AI voices), the same behaviors repeat:

- **They own a lane.** One recognizable topic territory (e.g., "AI alignment & safety," "AI governance & the AI Act," "building reliable agents"). The audience knows exactly what they'll get. This is Topic Authority in practice — and the company's lane is the seam where all three pillars meet, i.e. its positioning wedge (see the active profile's `social-tuning.md`).
- **They have a point of view, not just news.** They don't report that something happened — they tell you what it *means* and what they'd do. Opinion + reasoning travels; summaries don't.
- **They teach in frameworks.** Named, repeatable mental models (steps, laws, "the X playbook"). Frameworks get saved, screenshotted, and re-shared — which is exactly what the algorithm rewards.
- **They're consistent for years, not weeks.** Compounding is the whole game. The top voices post multiple times a week, every week.
- **They engage as much as they post.** They live in the comments — theirs and others'. Comments on bigger accounts is how most of them seeded their first audience.
- **They're personal and specific.** Real stories, real numbers, named failures. Founders who post first-person ("here's what we got wrong shipping X") out-perform corporate accounts massively.
- **They build in formats that reward depth** — carousels and explainer video — because their value is teachable.

### Archetypes to deploy
| Archetype | Who plays it | Content (mapped to pillars) |
|---|---|---|
| **The Founder-Visionary** | CEO/founder | POV on where the category is going; contrarian takes; "what we're building and why" (`<Pillar A>`, `<Pillar C>`) |
| **The Builder/Educator** | Eng leads, DevRel | How-it-works explainers, carousels, architecture, the technical proof under the wedge (`<Pillar A>`, `<Pillar C>`) |
| **The Policy Translator** | Product/policy/marketing | "What this rule / framework means for builders" (`<Pillar B>`) |
| **The Employee Advocates** | 10–50 staff | Reshare + add a personal line; comment on founder posts in first hour |

### Regional read: APAC vs US voices
- **US/Western voices** lean **opinion, hot takes, and direct CTAs.** Punchier, more "personal-brand," more willing to be contrarian. Audiences convert better on clear, direct asks.
- **APAC voices** lean **community, optimism, and visual storytelling.** Engagement jumps when content is **localized** (≈+40% when localized/multi-language) and when it uses **local idioms/context** (India: ≈+33% more shares with local business idioms). **Short-form video** over-indexes in APAC (≈+58% reach), and **multi-image** posts do unusually well. Tone is warmer and more relationship-first; aggressive self-promo lands worse.

**Implication for any company with a global ICP straddling both regions:** run a **dual-voice strategy** — keep the global thought-leadership in a punchy, POV-driven register for the US/Western feed, and produce a localized, more visual, community-framed variant (and timing) for APAC. Same insight, two wrappers.

## 10. Distribution mechanics: profiles, the page, and the first hour

**Personal > company, structurally.** Build the founder + a stable of employee voices. Company page = jobs, product launches, proof, and a reshare amplifier. Don't pour creative energy into page organic — it's throttled.

**Employee advocacy is the multiplier.** 10–50 employees resharing *with their own commentary* (not bare reposts) can amplify reach many times over the page alone. Make it easy: pre-write 2–3 caption options employees can personalize, and ask them to engage in the **first hour**.

**The first-60-minutes protocol (run this on every important post):**
1. Post at a peak slot for the target region.
2. Notify 5–10 advocates (a quiet Slack ping, *not* a "everyone like this" pod — keep it genuine and varied).
3. Author replies to every comment within the first 60–90 min, each reply ending in a question to extend the thread.
4. Author spends 15–20 min commenting on *other* big posts in-niche right after publishing (drives profile views back).
5. Do **not** edit the post in the first hour (editing can reset/limit distribution); fix typos after it matures.

## 11. Templates (copy, fill, ship)

### A. Hook templates (first line, <140 chars)
1. `Most [ICP role]s think [common belief about AI]. After [experience], I think the opposite.`
2. `[Specific number] + [surprising outcome]. Here's how.`
3. `[Big claim inside one of your pillars]. And almost nobody's ready for it.`
4. `We [shipped/tried/broke] [agent/AI thing]. Here's what actually happened.`
5. `If you're deploying agents and still [risky practice], read this.`
6. `"Trust me, the AI handled it" is not a control. Here's what is 👇`
7. `The [N] mistakes I see teams make with [agent trust / AI compliance] — and the fix for each.`
8. `Everyone's shipping agents. Almost no one can prove what they did.`
9. `[Question your ICP is quietly asking — e.g., "How do you audit an autonomous agent?"]`
10. `I read the [N]-page [AI Act / report] so you don't have to. [N] things that matter.`

*Pillar tie-ins:* every hook lands inside one pillar and names what that pillar demands — `<Pillar A>` → the proof it asks for ("prove it"); `<Pillar B>` → the rule it translates ("what this means for you"); `<Pillar C>` → the failure mode it exposes. Derive the three tie-ins once from the profile's `content_pillars` and reuse them.

### C. Carousel skeletons

**"N mistakes" deck**
```
Slide 1: [N] mistakes [ICP] make with [topic]
Slide 2: Why this is expensive (the stakes)
Slides 3–(N+2): one mistake per slide → the fix under it
```

### D. Comment templates (for daily engagement on others' posts)
- Add-value: `Great point on [X]. The piece I'd add: [specific insight from your domain]. We've seen [concrete example].`
- Respectful-counter: `Agree on [X] — though I'd push on [Y]. In [context], [your evidence] suggests [alternative].`
- Question that invites a reply: `This matches what we're seeing. Curious — how are you handling [specific edge case] when [condition]?`

(Keep comments substantive; 1–3 sentences with a real idea. Avoid "Great post! 🔥".)

### E. CTA bank (end your posts with one)
- "Which of these have you actually shipped — and what broke?"
- "What would you add to this list?"
- "Where do you land on this, and why?"
- "If you're building in [space], what's your biggest blocker right now?"
- "Agree or disagree? Tell me where I'm wrong."

### F. Employee-advocate reshare kit (give this to staff)
```
When [founder] posts, within the first hour:
1. React + leave a 1–2 sentence substantive comment (not "great post").
2. If it fits your network, reshare WITH your own line:
   Option A: "This maps to what I'm seeing in [your area]: [one line]."
   Option B: "The part that stuck with me: [quote]. Here's why it matters for [audience]."
Never copy-paste identical comments — varied, genuine engagement only.
```

## 12. Metrics — what to track (and ignore)

**Track (these reflect the 2026 algorithm):**
- **Dwell-adjacent proxies:** impressions-per-follower, and engagement *rate* (not raw count).
- **Saves and shares** (highest-intent signals; carousels should lead here).
- **Comment count + quality** (real threads vs one-word).
- **Follower growth rate** week-over-week, and **profile views** (leading indicator).
- **Per-format engagement rate** — confirm the carousel > video > text ranking *for your audience*.
- **Per-region/per-slot performance** — validate your two-clock schedule.

**Mostly ignore:** vanity like counts, and any single vendor's universal multiplier. Your own 20-post baseline is the only benchmark that matters.

**Cadence:** review every 2 weeks for the first quarter, then monthly. Kill formats/slots that underperform *your* baseline; double down on winners.
