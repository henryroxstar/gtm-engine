# LinkedIn Reply — form templates

Fill-in scaffolds for the two reply forms. These encode the universal mechanics (no body links,
substantive value, no "Great post!"); the *voice* always comes from `profiles/<active>/knowledge/
voice.md`. Templates are starting points — rewrite to the specific post, never paste a cell verbatim.

## Public comment (default)

The soft-sell ladder, 1–4 short sentences, no signature, link-light:

```
[Agree — restate their specific point/claim, not praise. For an authority: credit 2–3 specific points, "building on this".]
[Contribute — one true thing they didn't already say: example, nuance, lesson, data, a design principle, or a frontier insight.]
[Bridge — optional, ≤1 sentence, named OR veiled (carry the value with no names; "the primitives already exist").]
[Question — optional, only if it genuinely deepens the thread. No bait.]
```

Four reliable comment shapes (from the LinkedIn playbook §11.D — adapt to voice):

- **Add-value:** `Strong point on [X]. The piece I'd add: [specific insight from our domain]. We've
  seen [concrete, named example].`
- **Respectful counter:** `Agree on [X] — though I'd push on [Y]. In [context], [evidence] points to
  [alternative].`
- **Question that invites a reply:** `This matches what we're seeing. Curious how you're handling
  [specific edge case] when [condition]?`
- **Veiled bridge (no names):** `Strongest part is [their specific point]. The design call I'd add:
  [capability stated as a principle]. Sounds hard — but the primitives for it already exist.` —
  **zero** brand / product / standard names; for an expert audience or when the operator asks to keep
  it unnamed.

Rules: 1–3 sentences with a real idea. Avoid "Great post! 🔥". No outbound link in the comment body —
if a resource truly helps, suggest it as a follow-up reply to your own comment, not here. **When the
post had a long-form behind it, reply to the article's thesis, not the teaser** — and never re-explain
a point the author already made.

## DM / connection note (on request)

Warm because it references the post; ≤ 280 chars; one ask; signed from `PROFILE.email_signature`:

```
[Open on the specific post — "your post on [X] — the bit about [detail] landed."]
[Bridge — one line: what we build in the same space and why it connects.]
[Ask — one easy next step (a quick swap of notes, a link they can ignore, a question).]
— [signature name]
```

**Substantive DM (expert / thought-leader, ~150–200 words)** — when the value is the point and 280
chars can't carry it. The contribution *is* the warm opener:

```
[Credit 2–3 of their strongest specific points — genuine, not flattery; signals you read the full piece.]
[Contribute — the one idea that extends their framing or fills a gap they left open.]
[Bridge — optional, named or veiled (carry the capability as a design principle; "the primitives exist").]
[Ask — one soft, low-pressure next step (compare notes), not a meeting demand.]
— [signature name]
```

Don't force 280 when depth earns the reply; don't pad a light touch to look deep.

Persona adjustment (from voice.md), applied to either form:

| Persona | Angle |
|---|---|
| CEO / founder | business / deal risk, where the space is going |
| CTO | build-vs-buy, architecture fit |
| CISO / risk | audit / regulatory exposure (name the regulator) |
| Builder / architect | how it actually works, the control/identity seam |

## What kills a soft-sell reply (self-check fast-fails)

- Opens with a compliment instead of engaging the content.
- Drafted from the teaser when a long-form existed — anchored to the hook, not the real thesis.
- Re-explains a point the author already made (the classic teaser-only failure; reads as not having
  read them).
- Pitches before contributing value.
- Names the company / product / standard in a reply that was meant to stay **veiled**.
- Drops a link in the comment body.
- Cites a number, customer, or outcome that isn't in `case-studies.md` / `product.md` (fabrication).
- More than one ask, or an engagement-bait question.
