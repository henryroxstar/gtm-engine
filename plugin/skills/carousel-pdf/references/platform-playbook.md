# Platform Playbook — LinkedIn & X visual content

The **single authority** for platform mechanics and attention craft shared by the visual-content
skills (`carousel-pdf`, `carousel-auto`, `carousel-visuals`, `infographic-data`,
`infographic-handwritten`). Skills link here instead of restating these facts — when a platform
changes, update this file only. Evidence reviewed **2026-07**; re-verify the platform-mechanics
sections (§1, §2, §3) before major revisions — they decay fastest.

---

## 1 — LinkedIn document posts (carousels)

**Format facts**
- 4:5 portrait, 1080×1350 px — the most vertical feed real estate on mobile (~60% of sessions).
  1:1 is the safe alternative. Never A4 or 16:9 landscape (renders smallest in feed).
- Upload as a **flattened PDF** (≤100 MB, ≤300 pages — platform limits, not targets).
- Slide count: **8–10 sweet spot** (6 floor, 12 ceiling). Under 5 under-earns saves; past 12,
  ~60% of readers are gone before the final card.

**How distribution works**
- **Dwell time is the primary ranking signal** — every swipe adds dwell; a carousel earns 2–3 min
  vs 15–30 s for a text post. Documents are the top organic format (≈7% avg engagement,
  Socialinsider 2026) and earn a disproportionate share of saves.
- **Saves matter ~5× more than likes** for reach. Substantive comment threads beat reactions.
- Distribution is decided in the **first 60–90 minutes** on a small network test — post when the
  audience is awake, and reply to early comments (author replies compound reach).
- Personal profiles out-engage company pages by ~63% on carousels — plan distribution accordingly.

**Retention curve (design against it)**
- Expect ~70–80% of openers at card 3, ~50–60% at card 7, ~40–50% at card 10.
- Consequence: don't spend all the strong material by card 3, and **re-hook at card 4–5** — a
  surprising stat, a reframe, or a "most people stop here — the good part is next" beat.

## 2 — LinkedIn CTA compliance (post-March-2026 authenticity update)

LinkedIn now **semantically detects engagement bait** — "comment YES", reaction polls, and
follow-for-follow phrasing are suppressed. Two close modes:

- **Default close (compliant):** one-line recap of the takeaway + exactly **ONE** ask — *save*
  ("Save this for your next audit"), *follow* (one-line reason), or a **genuine question** that
  invites real replies. Plus an identity block (name/handle/brand mark) for recall. Never stack
  CTAs on one card.
- **Lead-magnet close (opt-in only):** "Comment [WORD] → I'll DM you [asset]" still out-performs
  link posts for lead capture but sits in LinkedIn's grey zone and the advantage is narrowing.
  Use only when the operator explicitly wants lead capture, at most **one lead-magnet post every
  3–4 weeks**, and fulfil DMs **manually** — automated DM fulfilment risks account-level
  penalties. Never pair with automation tools.
- **Link-in-first-comment is detected** ("bridge behaviour") and penalised like in-post links —
  don't recommend it. Route via profile featured section, or accept the link penalty knowingly on
  a conversion post.

## 3 — X (Twitter) image mechanics

**Display facts**
- **Single image:** 16:9 (1600×900) is the only ratio reliably full-bleed on mobile *and*
  desktop. 4:5 shows fully on mobile but is centre-cropped on desktop — if using portrait, keep
  the payload in the centre / top ~56% of the frame.
- **Organic carousels exist (since Apr 2026):** 2+ images render as a swipeable carousel on iOS
  and as a grid on web/Android. Rules: **uniform aspect ratio** across all images (1:1 or 4:5),
  **≤4 images**, centre-safe composition (grids crop from centre), **payoff image first**.
- **A dense infographic underperforms on X.** A thread of 3–5 posts with one simple visual each
  beats one dense poster — tall images get centre-cropped with tap-to-expand, which kills
  completion. If a data story has >5 stats, split it.
- Export **PNG** (text stays sharp through X's compression).

**Dark-mode duty (large share of X users run Dim/Lights-Out)**
- Always ship a **solid background** — never a transparent PNG (it inherits the timeline surface
  and breaks in one theme).
- Avoid pure #000 / #FFF edges (glare in one mode, blend in the other) — stay in the
  ~#121212 / ~#E0E0E0 zone at the extremes, and sanity-check the graphic against both a white and
  a near-black surround.

**What to optimise on X**
- Ranking weights (open-sourced): replies ≈13.5× a like, author-replying-back is the strongest
  single signal, bookmarks ≈10×, native media ≈2× vs text-only. So: end with a question worth
  answering, reply to every substantive response, and design for the bookmark ("keep this").
- **X is an authority channel for B2B, not lead-gen** (~13% of B2B social leads vs LinkedIn's
  ~80%). No DM funnels; the goal is being cited, bookmarked, and followed.
- Write **alt text** for every image — accessibility plus search discoverability.

## 4 — Hook craft (the cover/headline is ~80% of the outcome)

- **The 3-second test:** a stranger must answer *what is this / what's the point / is it for me*
  within 3 seconds of the cover or headline. Insight-stating beats topic-labelling —
  "Every agent looks like a ghost" not "Thoughts on agent identity".
- Cover headline: **3–8 words**, one message, high contrast, no clutter. It competes as a
  thumbnail in the feed, not as a page.
- Hooks with a **specific number** lift click-through ~36%; odd numbers read as more credible.
- Show the swipe affordance: arrow or "swipe →" plus a visible card counter.
- Budget reality: best-in-class creators spend ~half the total design effort on the cover. Draft
  **three options minimum** and pick against the 3-second test.

**Hook formula library** (extend, don't replace, the classic three):
1. **Factual gap** — "[Thing everyone has] doesn't have [thing it needs]. Yet."
2. **Contrarian** — "Everyone is [doing X]. Nobody is [doing what matters]."
3. **Numbered promise** — "[N] things your [stack/team/audit] is missing."
4. **Outcome + timeframe** — "[Customer/entity] fixed [problem] in [timeframe]. Here's how."
5. **Stop doing X** — "Stop [common practice]. Do [better thing] instead."
6. **How [known entity] does it** — "How [recognisable company/framework] handles [problem]."

## 5 — Swipe momentum (carousels)

- **Every card must earn the next swipe.** End each middle card with a forward pull: an open
  loop, a transition phrase ("Here's why →", "That's not the worst part."), or the next number
  in a visible sequence. A card that resolves everything is a stopping point.
- **≤50 words per card** (target 25–40). One idea per card — two ideas is two cards. Max 6–8
  words per line; break lines at phrase boundaries.
- **Final card = recap + ONE ask + identity** (see §2 close modes). Never end abruptly on
  content, and never stack asks.

## 6 — Legibility & accessibility floors (1080-px-wide canvas)

- Type scale: cover headline **72–96 px** · section headlines **60–72 px** · body **≥36 px** ·
  captions/footnotes **≥24 px — the absolute floor**. If text must shrink below the floor to
  fit, cut words, not point size.
- Safe margins: ≥60 px sides, ≥80 px top/bottom. On 9:16 story canvases keep the top/bottom
  ~250 px clear (platform UI); on 4:5 keep the bottom ~150 px clear of anything critical.
- Contrast: **WCAG AA — 4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text**, checked against the actual background
  (including over imagery — use a scrim).
- **One accent colour discipline:** grey/neutral everything, spend the single accent only on the
  point you want the eye to land on (empirically validated declutter-and-focus).
- Alt text: one plain sentence per image (what it shows + the takeaway), written into the
  publish package.

## 7 — Caption craft (LinkedIn)

- The caption drives **semantic topic classification**; the document drives dwell. Name the
  topic and entities plainly — write for the classifier as well as the reader.
- **First ~140 characters** (mobile fold) carry the hook — payoff promise in line 1–2, and never
  repeat the cover text verbatim.
- 100–200 words. Short lines. One thought per line.
- **Never restate the carousel/infographic's own content** — no itemized list of the same
  questions, controls, stats, or spectrum labels that are already visible on the cards. The
  caption's job is to earn the swipe (hook, personal stake, one added opinion, a question), not
  to pre-summarize the payoff. If a reader can get the whole framework from the caption alone,
  they have no reason to open the document — that's a caption bug, not a feature. Reference the
  framework by name/count ("his four-question framework", "the playbook's in the carousel") and
  stop there; let the cards carry the specifics.
- **Hashtags: 0–3, inline or one line, never stacked** — hashtag following is gone; stacks read
  as spam. (This supersedes any older 5–6-tag guidance.)
- Close with a genuine question to invite replies — not "comment YES".

## 8 — Data-graphic craft (infographics & stat cards)

- **One dominant number (BAN — Big Anchor Number).** Size is processed pre-attentively: a huge
  stat + a delta/context line beats any chart at thumbnail size. Every data graphic picks ONE
  number as the anchor; everything else supports it.
- Chart choice: **donut only for 2–4 categories** with the summary stat in the hole; **bar when
  comparison matters** (length beats angle for accuracy); big-number when there's one value.
  Never more than 5–6 slices; group the tail into "Other".
- **Direct-label on the chart — no legends.** Legends force eye travel that fails at scroll speed.
- **≤5–7 data points per graphic.** More than that: split into a carousel (LinkedIn) or a thread
  of simpler graphics (X).
- Headline states the insight, not the subject: "Agent traffic doubled in 90 days", not "Agent
  traffic statistics".

## 9 — AI image generation with in-image text (Nano-Banana-Pro-class models)

- Wrap every string that must render in **double quotes** in the prompt; describe layout
  **spatially** ("headline top-left", "stat anchor centred, dominant").
- **Short strings render at ~100% accuracy; long ones garble.** Keep any in-image label ≤8 words;
  paragraphs never go in the image — they go in the caption.
- **Numbers must be verified on every generation** — digit swaps (72% → 27%) are the
  characteristic failure. The vision accuracy-check against the spec is not optional.
- Arrows/callout mislabeling is a known failure mode — prefer position ("beside", "below") over
  arrows when the model must place annotations.
- After **one** corrected-prompt retry, stop: the durable artefact is the spec — recommend
  finishing dense text in a design tool (AI for layout + short display text, design tool for
  body copy) rather than burning further budget.
